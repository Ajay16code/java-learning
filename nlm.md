Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Natural Language Processing is a branch of Artificial Intelligence that enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in text or speech form.


---

Definition

NLP combines linguistics and machine learning to allow systems to process human language and extract meaning from it.


---

Common Applications

Chatbots and virtual assistants

Language translation systems

Sentiment analysis of reviews and social media

Spam email detection

Text summarization

Speech recognition



---

Key Concepts

1. Tokenization

Breaking text into smaller units such as words or sentences.
Example: "I love AI" becomes ["I", "love", "AI"]

2. Stop Word Removal

Removing common words that do not add much meaning.
Example: "I am learning AI" becomes "learning AI"

3. Stemming and Lemmatization

Reducing words to their base or root form.
Example: "running" becomes "run"

4. Named Entity Recognition (NER)

Identifying entities such as names, locations, and organizations in text.
Example: "Elon Musk founded Tesla"
Person: Elon Musk
Organization: Tesla

5. Sentiment Analysis

Determining whether text expresses positive, negative, or neutral sentiment.
